Tim Berners-Lee

The creator of HTML and the World Wide Web

A Short Summary of Sir Tim's Life

Sir Tim Berners-Lee is a British computer scientist renowned as the inventor of the World Wide Web. Born in London in 1955, Berners-Lee developed the foundational concepts and protocols for the web while working at CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research, in the late 1980s and early 1990s. In 1989, he proposed the idea of a hypertext system to facilitate information sharing among researchers. This led to the creation of the first web browser, editor, and server software, as well as the implementation of the HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol) and HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) standards. In 1991, Berners-Lee launched the first website, which outlined the basic principles of the World Wide Web and provided information on how to create web pages. His visionary work democratized access to information, revolutionizing communication, collaboration, and commerce on a global scale. Berners-Lee's commitment to an open and decentralized web has shaped the digital landscape and earned him numerous accolades, including a knighthood, the Turing Award, and the Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering. His ongoing efforts through organizations like the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) continue to advance the web's development and ensure its accessibility to all.

A Timeline of his Important Milestones

  • 1955 Jun 8 - Born London as Timothy John Berners-Lee
  • 1973 - Graduated from Queen's College, Oxford, receiving a first bachelor's degree of arts in physics
  • 1980 - Started working at CERN as a freelance consultant for the physics research facility
  • 1989 Mar 5 - Wrote the first proposal for the World Wide Web, labeled as a "large hyperlink database with typed links"
  • 1990 Oct 1 - Started working on the World Wide Web
  • 1991 Aug 6 - The first website is created
  • 1993 Jan 23 - Mosaic Web Brower is launched, the first application to make the internet accessible
  • 1994 Oct 1 - The World Wibe Web Consortium, a standards organization for the internet, is established
  • 1997 Jan 7 - The final update and later termination of Mosaic
  • 1999 Nov 11 - Sir Tim appears on the Time's 100 Most Influential List of the 20th century
  • 2004 Jul 16 - Knighted by the Order of the British Empire, officially referred to as Sir Tim Berners-Lee
  • 2004 - He begins teaching as a professor of computer science at the University of Southampton
  • 2009 Jun 1 - Berners-Lee is requested to make online data more safe for the public by the UK government, and is therefore employed by the government
  • 2009 Nov 1 - The World Wide Web Foundation is established
  • 2012 May 1 - He becomes the president of the Open Data Institute
  • 2013 Oct 10 - The Alliance for Affordable Internet (A4AI) is launched including partners such as: Google, Facebook, Intel, and Microsoft
